Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct (v2.1)

Last updated: 16 June 2018

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

1. Purpose

Our community is dedicated to providing a welcoming and respectful experience for everyone. This code of conduct outlines expected behaviour and steps for reporting unacceptable behaviour.

2. Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, project discussions, and public events organized by the project.

3. Community Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment for the community include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.
- Showing empathy towards other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour by participants include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

4. Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported by contacting the project team at `dev@aidanlowson.com`. All reports will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

The maintainers who decide on enforcement actions are: the repository owner and designated maintainers. Enforcement actions may include:

- A private warning to the offender.
- Temporary or permanent banning from project spaces.
- Removal of the offending content.
- Other actions as appropriate.

5. Reporting Guidelines

When reporting an incident, please include:

- Your contact information (email preferred).
- A description of the behaviour, including dates, times, and locations if available.
- Names of any participants and witnesses.
- Screenshots or logs if available.

Reports should be sent to `dev@aidanlowson.com`. Reports will be handled with discretion and appropriate follow-up.
